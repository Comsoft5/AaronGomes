How I Think

My Approach to Product

01

Talk to users first and understand gaps

I've been wrong about what users want more times than I can count. The fastest way to be less wrong is to get in front of them early and often — before spending too much resources.

02

Earn the right to your opinion

Early in my career I've learned that good instincts matter less than good evidence. I try to back my ideas with research, not just intuition — and stay genuinely open to being constructive critisism.

03

Scope is a decision, not a given

The most underrated product skill is saying no — or at least "not yet." Keeping scope tight forces clarity about what actually matters and lets teams move faster and learn sooner. I've found that it is far too easy to try an do too much too soon

04

Write things down

Clarity of writing reflects clarity of thinking. A well-written brief builds alignment and provides the context to make good decisions.

05

Relationships make products

Almost everything gets done through trust, communication, and mutual respect. I invest in relationships across departments and with external stakeholders as they're the glue that holds the product execution together.

06

Stay a student

There's a lot I don't know yet, and I'm okay with that. I read, reassess, seek out feedback actively, and treat every project as a chance to get better.
